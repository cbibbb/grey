Project Purpose

This website was created to inform people living in Middle Tennessee about food insecurity. Food insecurity means that a person or family does not always have reliable access to enough affordable and nutritious food. This issue connects to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 2: Zero Hunger.

What It Means

Food insecurity is not just hunger. It is the ongoing stress of not knowing if there will be enough food for the next meal.

Why It Happens

Rising food prices, low wages, transportation barriers, and food deserts all make food access harder for many families.

How to Help

People can help by donating, volunteering, supporting food banks, reducing food waste, and sharing local resources.

Why This Matters Locally

Food insecurity affects families, children, college students, and older adults across Middle Tennessee. Communities such as Nashville, Murfreesboro, and surrounding rural areas all face challenges related to access, affordability, and nutrition.

By learning about this issue, people can better understand how poverty, transportation, education, and public health are connected.
