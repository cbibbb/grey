What is Food Insecurity?

Understanding the difference between hunger and food insecurity in Middle Tennessee.

Definition

Food insecurity happens when individuals or families do not have consistent access to enough affordable and nutritious food. It means people may not know where their next meal is coming from or may have to choose between buying food and paying for other basic needs like rent, gas, or healthcare.

Hunger vs Food Insecurity

Hunger is the physical feeling of discomfort caused by lack of food. Food insecurity is the larger issue that causes hunger because people do not have reliable access to meals over time.

Who It Affects

Food insecurity impacts children, college students, working families, senior citizens, and people living in both urban and rural communities throughout Middle Tennessee.

Why Awareness Matters

Many people assume food insecurity only affects homeless individuals, but many families facing this issue may have jobs, homes, and children. Raising awareness helps communities better understand the problem and support local solutions.
